Two products, one philosophy

Pick your format

ReelGen

Topic → edited stock-footage reel

An LLM plans the storyboard (hook, beats, closer). CLIP semantically matches every beat to real footage — scored on sharpness, palette and motion before the best clip wins. FFmpeg does actual color science, Ken Burns motion and mood-matched transitions. Animated text that looks IG-native, not watermarked-app.

python reelgen.py generate \
  --topic "Hidden cafes in Tokyo" \
  --mood calm --style warm-analog
Brainrot

Mood → narrated gameplay reel

An LLM writes an 8–20s reddit-style micro-story. Kokoro-82M narrates it locally, for free. faster-whisper timestamps every word, karaoke captions get burned in over randomly-sliced gameplay footage. The format that floods Shorts — except yours costs nothing to make.

python brainrot.py generate \
  --mood funny --telegram
Under the hood

How a Brainrot reel happens

1

Story. LLM writes a punchy first-person micro-story in two synced versions: one spelled out for the TTS engine, one formatted for on-screen captions.

2

Voice. Kokoro-82M (Apache 2.0, runs on CPU) narrates. Three narrator voices, zero API cost.

3

Alignment. faster-whisper transcribes the audio for timestamps — trusted for timing, never for spelling — then timings snap onto the known script text.

4

Captions. Real ASS subtitles with word-level karaoke highlight, burned in by libass. Not frame-by-frame image hacks.

5

Edit. Random non-repeating gameplay slices, concatenated to the narration's exact length, cropped to 1080×1920 — one ffmpeg pass.

6

Review. Finished reel lands in your Telegram. No auto-posting, by design.

No hype

The honest part

  • Renders take 1–3 minutes per video on CPU — this is a batch tool, not a realtime toy.
  • Free LLM tiers are tight (~20 videos/day on Gemini's free key). Volume needs a paid key.
  • Gameplay footage isn't included — bring footage you have the rights to use.
  • Good editing doesn't guarantee views. Posting cadence and trends still matter more than the cut.
